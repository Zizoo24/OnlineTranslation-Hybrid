Navigating the ICP Requirements

Applying for a UAE Golden Visa is a significant milestone. Whether you're a high-skilled professional, investor, or exceptional talent, the Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship (ICP) requires specific documentation that must be translated by MOJ-licensed translators.

We understand the anxiety around rejection. A single formatting error or missing attestation can delay your entire application. That's why we've developed the Equivalency Pack—a comprehensive solution that handles not just translation, but the entire compliance chain.

The Equivalency Process

We guide you through each step of the credential verification chain

1

Document Review

We assess your documents and identify any attestation gaps. Common issue: UK degrees often need Apostille before UAE acceptance.

2

MOJ Translation

Your documents are translated by our Ministry of Justice licensed translators, using ICP-specific terminology.

3

MOFA Attestation

We coordinate digital or physical attestation through the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and International Cooperation.

4

MOE Equivalency

For degree holders, we assist with Ministry of Education equivalency—ensuring your qualification is recognized in UAE.

5

ICP Submission Ready

All documents formatted and named correctly for ICP portal upload. We provide a checklist to ensure nothing is missed.

Golden Visa FAQ

The ICP requires salary certificates to include specific terminology: job title, start date, current salary in AED, and a statement that the position is "permanent." We format your translation to match these exact requirements, reducing rejection risk.
